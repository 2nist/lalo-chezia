# Chord Progressions & Harmony
> Based on *2-5-1 Progressions* reference document

---

## Core Concept: Harmonic Functions

Every chord in a key serves one of three primary **functions** — Tonic, Subdominant, or Dominant. These create the motion of tension → release that drives all tonal music.

| Function | Chords | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Tonic (T)** | I, vi | Stability — "home base" |
| **Pre-Dominant (PD)** | ii, IV | Motion — leads toward tension |
| **Dominant (D)** | V, vii° | Tension — wants to resolve to I |

> **Classic arc:** T → PD → D → T (e.g., C → F → G → C)

---

## The Scale Degrees & Their Personalities

| Degree | Name | Chord (C Major) | Function | Feel |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| I | Tonic | C Major | Home | Stable, resolved |
| ii | Supertonic | D Minor | Pre-dominant | Smooth, jazzy |
| iii | Mediant | E Minor | Weak tonic sub | Introspective |
| IV | Subdominant | F Major | Pre-dominant | Moving away from home |
| V | Dominant | G Major | Tension | Urgently wants to resolve |
| vi | Submediant | A Minor | Tonic substitute | Softer, melancholic |
| vii° | Leading Tone | B Diminished | Strong tension | Most unstable, pulls hard to I |

## Common Chord Progressions

### Major Key Progressions
| Name | Progression | Feel/Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Pop Axis** | I – V – vi – IV | Ubiquitous pop/rock |
| **50s / Doo-Wop** | I – vi – IV – V | Nostalgic, classic |
| **Jazz ii-V-I** | ii – V – I | Smooth resolution, jazz standard |
| **Circle Progression** | vi – ii – V – I | Circular, jazz |
| **Pachelbel's Canon** | I – V – vi – iii – IV – I – IV – V | Baroque, lush |
| **12-Bar Blues** | I – I – I – I – IV – IV – I – I – V – IV – I – V | Blues foundation |
| **Rhythm Changes** | I – vi – ii – V | Jazz standard base |
| **Montgomery-Ward Bridge** | I – IV – ii – V | Country, resolving |

### Minor Key Progressions
| Name | Progression | Feel |
|---|---|---|
| **Natural minor** | i – VII – VI – VII | Rock, folk |
| **Folia** | i – V – i – ♭VII – ♭III – ♭VII – i – V | Baroque, haunting |
| **Aeolian rock** | i – VI – III – VII | Power, anthem rock |

### Mixed / Chromatic
| Name | Progression | Feel |
|---|---|---|
| **Backdoor** | ii – ♭VII7 – I | Mysterious, jazz |
| **Tritone sub** | ii – ♭II7 – I | Chromatic, modern jazz |
| **Mixolydian vamp** | I – ♭VII – IV | Bluesy, rock |

## Cadences (Ending Progressions)

| Type | Progression | Feel |
|---|---|---|
| **Perfect Authentic** | V – I (root position, melody ends on 1) | Strongest resolution |
| **Imperfect Authentic** | V – I (inverted or melody not on 1) | Resolution, but softer |
| **Plagal ("Amen")** | IV – I | Gentle, gospel-like |
| **Half Cadence** | ? – V | Open-ended, expectant |
| **Deceptive** | V – vi | Surprise — lands on vi instead of I |
| **Phrygian** | iv6 – V (minor key) | Spanish/Baroque flavor |
| **Picardy Third** | v – I (major I in minor key) | Hopeful ending in minor |

## The 7 Modes

| Mode | Scale Degrees | Character | Typical Chord Move |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ionian** (Major) | 1-2-3-4-5-6-7 | Bright, happy | I – IV – V |
| **Dorian** | 1-2-♭3-4-5-6-♭7 | Minor but jazzy, raised 6th | i – IV – VII |
| **Phrygian** | 1-♭2-♭3-4-5-♭6-♭7 | Dark, Spanish, exotic | i – ♭II |
| **Lydian** | 1-2-3-♯4-5-6-7 | Dreamy, floating | I – II – V |
| **Mixolydian** | 1-2-3-4-5-6-♭7 | Bluesy, dominant feel | I – ♭VII – IV |
| **Aeolian** (Natural Minor) | 1-2-♭3-4-5-♭6-♭7 | Sad, dark | i – iv – v |
| **Locrian** | 1-♭2-♭3-4-♭5-♭6-♭7 | Highly unstable, dissonant | Rarely used as tonic |

## Modal Interchange (Borrowed Chords)

Borrowing from the **parallel minor** (or other modes) adds color and emotional weight.

| Borrowed Chord | From Mode | Effect in C Major |
|---|---|---|
| iv (Fm) | Aeolian | Melancholic, emotional shift |
| ♭III (E♭) | Aeolian | Dramatic, bluesy |
| ♭VI (A♭) | Aeolian | Cinematic, surprising |
| ♭VII (B♭) | Mixolydian | Rock, bluesy, modal |
| ♭II (D♭) — Neapolitan | Phrygian | Classical tension, dramatic |

## Chord Substitutions Quick Reference

| Original | Substitute | Why It Works | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| I (C) | vi (Am) | Share 2 common tones | Softer tonic |
| IV (F) | ii (Dm) | Share 2 common tones | Jazzier, smoother |
| V (G7) | ♭II7 (D♭7) — Tritone Sub | Same tritone (B–F) | Chromatic, jazz |
| V (G) | ♭VII (B♭) | Modal borrowing | Bluesy, rock |
| V (G) | ii – V (Dm – G) | Adds pre-dominant | Smooth jazz |
| ii (Dm) | ♭II (D♭) — Neapolitan | Chromatic pre-dominant | Dramatic, filmic |
| IV (F) | iv (Fm) | Modal interchange | Dark, emotional |

## Harmonic Transitions in Songwriting

### Verse → Chorus
- **V → I:** Strong lift, forward momentum *(e.g., Nirvana — Smells Like Teen Spirit)*
- **IV → I:** Softer resolution *(e.g., Beatles — Let It Be)*
- **vi → I:** Minor to major lift — emotional brightness *(e.g., Adele — Someone Like You)*
- **ii → V → I:** Pre-chorus tension build *(e.g., Bon Jovi — Livin' on a Prayer)*

### Chorus → Bridge
- **Modulate to relative minor or distant key:** Dramatic contrast *(Queen — Bohemian Rhapsody)*
- **Deceptive cadence V → vi:** Introspective, melancholic *(Beatles — Hey Jude)*
- **Chromatic pivot ♭VII or ♭VI:** Unexpected but smooth *(Radiohead — Creep)*

### Bridge → Final Chorus
- **Key change (half/whole step up):** Climactic lift *(Whitney Houston — I Will Always Love You)*
- **Extended V pedal:** Tension-filled lead-in *(Journey — Don't Stop Believin')*

### Outro
- **IV → I (Plagal vamp):** Gospel "amen" feel *(Beatles — Hey Jude)*
- **Fade on repeating loop:** Open-ended, dreamlike *(Prince — Purple Rain)*
- **Picardy Third:** Minor key resolves to major I — hopeful *(Beatles — A Day in the Life)*

## Uncommon Harmonic Transitions

| Technique | What It Is | Notable Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Parallel Mode Shift** | Major ↔ minor on same root | Beatles — *While My Guitar Gently Weeps* |
| **Common-Tone Modulation** | Shared note pivots to new key | Radiohead — *There, There* |
| **Chromatic Mediant** | Jump by major/minor 3rd | Beatles — *Golden Slumbers* |
| **V → ♭VI** | Dominant to flat-six instead of I | Radiohead — *Paranoid Android* |
| **Neapolitan Pivot** | ♭II as pivot to new key | Queen — *Bohemian Rhapsody* |
| **Tritone Modulation** | Jump to key a tritone away | Rush — *YYZ* |
| **Lydian Modulation** | I → II instead of V | Fleetwood Mac — *Dreams* |
| **Parallel Dominant Planing** | Dom 7ths moving in parallel | Jimi Hendrix — *Freedom* |

## Harmony Systems Summary

| System | Definition | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Diatonic** | Only notes within one key | Pop, folk, rock — clean & predictable |
| **Functional** | Chords assigned roles (T-PD-D) | Classical, jazz — directed motion |
| **Modal** | Based on modes, avoids V-I | Jazz, folk, experimental — color & ambiguity |
| **Tonal** | Full Western classical system | Orchestral, film, classical — structured |
